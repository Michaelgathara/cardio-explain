Telemetry Simulator : Atrial Fibrillation

Look and Listen!

Fibrillatory (f’) waves are often seen as oscillating baseline comprised of irregular spikes, while the irregular ventricular rhythm will be seen as irregular intervals between QRS complexes and heard as irregular QRS beeping


Atrial fibrillation increases the risk for stroke 5-fold!

Atrial fibrillation (AFib) is a chaotic atrial rhythm represented by fibrillatory (f’) waves (irregular spikes) of varying amplitude, morphology and duration. This results in a random oscillating baseline on the ECG and an irregular ventricular rhythm represented by irregularly spaced QRS complexes.


f' = fibrillatory waves
QRS = ventricular contraction
