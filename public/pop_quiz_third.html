Atrial Fibrillation : Find the imposter

Instructions: Click on the ECG strip that does not show atrial fibrillation

On ECG, atrial fibrillation is represented by (1) fibrillatory (f’) waves of varying amplitude, duration and morphology producing random oscillations of the baseline, and (2) an irregular ventricular rhythm.(irregular intervals between QRS complexes). f’ waves originate from multiple atrial foci firing at the same time, like fireworks going off (see figure, top right). Both criteria are demonstrated in the top and bottom rhythm strips.


In contrast, if you look carefully at the middle strip, P waves are present (small regular upright waves preceding each spike/QRS complex; arrows). This indicates that the presence of a sinus rhythm. The middle strip also shows QRS complexes at a regular rate, another finding not found in atrial fibrillation. In this example, the rate is ~ 120 bpm, making the rhythm sinus tachycardia.

IMAGES HERE WHEN THE QUALITY IMPROVES

f' = fibrillatory waves
P = P waves originating sinus node
QRS = ventricular contraction
