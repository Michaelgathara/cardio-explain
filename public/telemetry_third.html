Telemetry Simulator : Third Degree AV Block

Look and Listen!

In a third-degree AV block, independent P and QRS waves are a hallmark, with atrial and ventricular rhythms each following their own path. This disconnection between the atria and ventricles will be seen as unrelated and regular intervals between both P waves and QRS complexes, and heard as a dual-beat rhythm with separate atrial and ventricular contractions.


Third-degree AV block is a serious condition that can lead to cardiac arrest if not treated immediately!

Third-degree atrioventricular (AV) block, also known as complete heart block, is characterized by the complete disconnection between atrial and ventricular rhythms. This is represented by independent P and QRS waves on the ECG, where atrial and ventricular contractions occur at their own, separate, inherent rates. This results in a fixed yet uncorrelated rhythm, demonstrated by regularly spaced P waves and QRS complexes, but without a consistent relationship between them. The lack of synchrony is also reflected by a random PR interval duration since the atria and ventricles beat independently of each other.
